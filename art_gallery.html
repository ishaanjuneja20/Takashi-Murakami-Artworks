Graduation Cover Art

  1. This artwork was created by Takashi Murakami for Kanye's 2007 album "Graduation"
  2. It represents the rapper Kanye West as a bear, when he finally graduated from his college
  3. The album was released in 2007 and the cover was made in 2006

The artwork for Kanye West's Graduation album was created in 2007 by Japanese contemporary artist Takashi Murakami, coinciding with the album's release that same year. Murakami's distinctive style is featured prominently in the cover art, which was a significant cultural moment in music.

Kids See Ghosts Cover Art

  1. This artwork was created by Takashi Murakami for Kanye's 2018 album "Kids See Ghosts"
  2. The cover is a modernized version of Murakami's 2001 woodblock print "Mani Fuji"
  3. The cover showcases Murakami's "superflat" style, a blend of traditional Japanese art (like Nihonga) and contemporary pop art.

The Kids See Ghosts album cover is an artwork by Takashi Murakami, featuring a vibrant, psychedelic landscape inspired by Hokusai's traditional Japanese woodblock print "Mani Fuji". Key elements include a Mount Fuji backdrop, Murakami's trademark "Oval Characters" confronting the viewer, and Chinese characters translating to "Chaos".

MBDTF Cover Art

  1. This artwork is one of the 8 artworks designed for Kanye's album MBDTF.
  2. The cover shows West being straddled on a couch by an armless winged female, who has fearsome features and a long, spotted tail.
  3. It was designed on 10 October, 2010.

The various covers explore themes of celebrity, fame, and Kanye's self-reflection on how it has shaped him, including both the excesses and downfalls of notoriety. The album's art reflects its maximalist production and opulent aesthetic, with influences from classical art, surrealism, and cubism.

    Steps to create your own art
